Ironman World Championship

Game of skill - game of luck

Although the Ironman Championship for women has been organized already 42 times and 123 medals have been given, there has been only 52 distinct athletes that were on the podium throughout all the competitions.

Isn't that interesting?

Let's look at the story behind it.

Let's look at all the medalists and years when they participated. The athletes are sorted chronogically, according to their first appearance on the Ironman podium.

It is immediately visible that some athletes are much more successful than the others. When they enter a competition, they keep on winning the medals for a couple of years in the row. Let's mark the ones who won more than 5 medals.

Now, if you look closer at the most-winning athletes, you will notice something peculiar: sometimes, they did not participate in the competition. This may normally happen for different reasons, but why would so many exceptional, winning athletes systematically skip the competition?

There is one very probable reason for this. Not everyone knows that for a long time the participation in Ironman competition used to be determined by the lottery.

This means that some perfectly prepared athletes were not able to participate, therefore someone else could win the medal they would have gotten otherwise. And as the data often shows, when the missing athlete reappears in the following year, she claims back her winning position.

This makes Ironman a very peculiar competition: one demanding extreme physical fitness, but also one where the results are strongly influenced by a random chance. May the best win—but only among those who had enough luck to participate.
